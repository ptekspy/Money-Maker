## The objective

The first campaign is designed to produce:

- three live landlord pilots;
- one paying founding customer after Stripe is active; and
- one distribution partner willing to introduce LetDue to its landlords.

The first batch is deliberately small. Every message is personalised and every reply is handled by a person. No scraped personal email addresses, bulk mail or automated LinkedIn spam.

## Who buys and who distributes

LetDue currently monitors up to three properties for £29 a year. The natural buyer is therefore a self-managing landlord with one to three properties.

Letting agents, HMO managers, certificate suppliers and landlord associations are not being treated as ordinary £29 customers. They are routes to those customers. We will ask them to offer a free deadline audit, refer suitable landlords, or give a short educational demonstration. Selected operators can test three properties so we learn what a future portfolio plan must include.

## Channel order

1. Email public generic business inboxes at clearly incorporated organisations.
2. Use dedicated partnership or recognised-service-provider forms.
3. Message community organisers privately to request permission for an educational post or short clinic.
4. Publish helpful posts only after organiser approval.

Do not cold-email private landlords or sole traders from harvested lists. Do not message community members individually. Maintain a suppression list for every opt-out.

## Offer

The opening offer is a **free three-property founding pilot**:

- upload or enter existing certificate dates;
- receive a clear deadline calendar;
- test the reminder flow;
- no card required during the pilot; and
- give 15 minutes of candid feedback.

After Stripe is active, a useful pilot is invited onto the £29-per-year founding plan. A partner can instead offer its audience the free deadline audit at `https://letdue.com`.

## Reply handling

**Interested:** propose two specific 15-minute slots, ask whether they are testing as a landlord or referring clients, and send only the normal LetDue URL.

Use this response:

> Brilliant — thank you. You can start without a card at https://letdue.com. Run the free audit with one real property, then choose **Start free 14-day pilot**. Bookmark the private dashboard it creates; you can add two more properties from there. If you prefer, send me two suitable 15-minute times and I will stay available while you set up the first one.

After activation, record the email, organisation, pilot start date, pilot end date, number of properties added, documents uploaded, first useful outcome and any obstacle. Never ask the prospect to email certificate files; they should upload them through the private dashboard.

**What does it cost?:** “The founding plan is £29 a year for up to three properties. The pre-launch pilot is free because I need honest product feedback before asking you to pay.”

**We already use property software:** “That makes sense. LetDue is intentionally much smaller: a certificate-specific tool for landlords who do not need—or do not consistently use—a full management suite. I would still value your view on whether it fills that gap.”

**Is this legal advice / a compliance guarantee?:** “No. LetDue organises documents and reminders; it does not certify a property, guarantee compliance or replace professional advice.”

**Not interested:** thank them once, record the opt-out and never follow up.

**Automatic reply:** record it but do not count it as a positive response. Follow the stated response window unless the auto-response says the team is unavailable; never use an urgent phone or WhatsApp route for a non-urgent partnership pitch.

## Campaign controls

- Send no more than eight initial messages on the first day.
- Split them into four pilot invitations and four partner invitations.
- Never claim that LetDue guarantees compliance.
- Never imply council, association or professional-body endorsement.
- Record the source URL, date sent, response and opt-out state.
- Pause the script after 20 contacts and rewrite it based on actual replies.
